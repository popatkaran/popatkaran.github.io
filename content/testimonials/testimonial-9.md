When he joined, the relationship between product and engineering was adversarial. Product saw engineering as a black box that said no to everything. Engineering saw product as a source of unrealistic demands with no understanding of technical constraints.

He rebuilt that relationship by making technical constraints visible and understandable — not as blockers, but as design inputs. He introduced architecture reviews that included product stakeholders, explained trade-offs in business terms, and consistently found ways to deliver the business outcome even when the original technical approach was not feasible. He is the reason our product and engineering teams now work as one.
