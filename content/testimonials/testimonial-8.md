We asked him to help us adopt AI-assisted development across a team of 12 engineers. Most AI tooling initiatives I have seen produce a spike of enthusiasm and then fade when the novelty wears off. His approach was different.

He started by understanding how each engineer actually worked, then identified the specific friction points where AI assistance would have the highest impact. He embedded the practices into our existing workflow — code review, spec writing, debugging — so they became habits, not experiments. Six months later, our team's output has measurably increased and the tools are still in daily use.
