We brought him in for a cloud cost review after our AWS bill had grown 3x in 18 months without a corresponding growth in traffic. He identified four architectural patterns that were generating unnecessary cost — cross-AZ data transfer in hot paths, NAT Gateway routing for internal AWS service calls, over-provisioned RDS instances for workloads that fit DynamoDB, and synchronous fan-out in our API layer.

He proposed fixes for each, prioritised by impact and implementation risk, and we implemented them in six weeks. The savings paid for his engagement in the first month. The architectural improvements paid dividends for much longer.
