We hired him at Series A to design the architecture that would take us through Series B and beyond. At the time, we were processing 50,000 orders per month. Eighteen months later, we were processing 500,000 — and the architecture he designed handled that 10x increase without a single architectural change.

He made decisions that seemed conservative at the time — event sourcing for our order domain, read replicas from day one, async processing for everything non-critical — that turned out to be exactly right. He was not designing for where we were. He was designing for where we were going. That is the difference between a good engineer and a great architect.
